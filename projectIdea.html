Studio Gym

Overview

A mobile phone application together with a web page to create and store statistical information concerning a persons workout routine. Currently there are many professional looking applications out there that already cater to this market.

However, none provide much information on understanding how much a given workout is actually providing. Much of the focus is on giving the user preset workouts, lots of fancy videos on how to perform the said exercises. Little emphasis is placed on the interpretation of the data collected. There are many heart monitors and distance tracking tools, but none provide much beyond basic, time taken, sets, reps, distance travelled etc.

Therefore, this would be a Personal training application to store client workout information and provide a more comprehensive analyse of the data produced. The analysis will provide an understanding of the muscle groups the client is focusing on and to what extend they are being worked. Comparisons in the form of ratios will be produced to ascertain the level to which each muscle and the opposing muscle are being worked. This would be used to produce a ratio and from this a variance or difference.

Personal trainers and or Physiotherapist could then use this data to ascertain the acceptable balance levels between muscles.

Motivation

When I first started working out in a gym one of the single things that I wanted was an enormous chest and shoulders. Hence, like many prospective, male, gym enthusiasts, I would focus mainly on exercises, such as, Bench Press, Shoulder Press, and the occasional Bicep Curl or Beach Curl. This would usually result in very little exercise being performed on anything else.

The negative aspects of this type of training would lead to injury. The under worked muscles, would become proportionately weaker, creating the classic 'Hunchback look'. In extreme cases these imbalances can lead to serious tendon, ligament, and muscle damage.

So, how do we determine when this is occurring and to what extent? And, more importantly how can it be avoided? Usually, a good Personal Trainer, or a Physiotherapist would be able to recognise the signs and direct the person appropriately.

However, for most of us, this still does not register. Being able to produce quantifiable data and produce a graphical representation of a persons workout to highlight any imbalances between muscle groups would address this issue better.

Detailed Description

If I perform a Bicep Curl exercise using a Dumbbell weighing 30Kg. It is said, that I have performed, one repetition, or 30Kg's of work on my bicep muscle. Taking this further, and repeating it ten times, would result in 10 Repetitions at 30Kg's. Thus, having performed 300Kg's of work (10 Reps * 30Kgs). If I then perform the opposing exercise, a Tricep Extension, and performed the same number of repetitions at the same weight as the Bicep Curl. I have then, also performed 300Kg's of work, but on my triceps.

The bicep has performed 300Kgs of work and the Tricep has also performed 300Kgs of work, a ratio can be drawn of 300/300 between the two opposing muscles.

Many such opposing muscle groups exist in the human body and all can be analysed in this way, creating many ratios, to produce an overall understanding of how well balanced a given workout is progressing.

From here a percentage of the work done for each exercise can be calculated as a percentage of the total work done.

The main objective can then be achieved by creating a setup to match a muscle to its opposing muscle. A ratio can be produced as well as a variance based on this ratio. As can be seen the Variance between biceps and triceps is quite large indicating the person has been focusing on triceps more than their biceps. The variance between Middle Pectoral muscles and the Latissimus Dorsi is also large. Indicating the chest muscles have been over worked in relation to the back muscles.

Outcome

I do not expect to replace the current exercise programs out in the market place. However, this should be viewed as a step towards a singularly, more complex, method of analysing workout data than what is currently available.

Bearing this in mind, the people most likely to begin uptake of such a product would need to be Personal Trainers with a reasonable understanding of the technicalities and complexities involved. It would require a person whom has the patience and enthusiasm to want to work with myself and others to build such an interface.

This is not for the average gym attendee at first.

I view this more as an exercise to demonstrate and learn the skills required to build such a project. I selected this as it would require a fairly high level of complexity, with and containing a logical continuity that would facilitate an ever growing exercise orientated application. In short, something challenging.

I am also concerned about potential problems, in particular, this could lead to litigation if somebody claims that this future product caused an injury. I am not sure of all the complexities here. What my obligations would be and duty of care to a user would be. This is potentially a reason to avoid proceeding into the market place with such a product.

Note : I would prefer to build a game that is pretty much the same as every other game out there. I would love to be a game developer. I see this project as a way to learn and develop something complicated to meet that end, which ever that happens to be.
